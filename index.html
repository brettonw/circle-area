The intersection of two circles of varying radii is an interesting geometric problem. I am interested in the solution so that I can incorporate a shadow umbra/penumbra calculation into a shader program.

The algorithm divides the solution into three independent ranges:

  • Disjoint: if the two circles are further apart than the sum of their radii, there are no points of intersection, and the answer is simply related to the area of the circle you care about.

  • Contained: if one of the two circles is completely contained within the other, there are no points of intersection, and the answer is simply related to the areas of the two circles.

  • Intersecting: Technically, there could be one, two, or infinitely many points of intersection. The one-point case is when the edges of the circles are touching. The infinitely-many-points case is when the circles are exactly the same size and perfectly centered on each other. I model both of these situations as either Contained or Disjoint because I really only care about overlapping area.

    The overlapping area case is solved by computing the lens shape at the end of a right triangle wedge on each of the circles, and summing them. There are plenty of other references for this on the web if you need the derivation.
